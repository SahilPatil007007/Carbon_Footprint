Recommendation on how to reduce
your carbon footprint

In addition, we have provided a list of useful tips to help you reduce your carbon footprint. These are generic and are not linked to the answers given in the carbon calculator.

Transport and Recreation

1. Join a car club or car sharing scheme.

2. Consider replacing your car with a low emission vehicle.

3. For short journeys, walk or cycle.

4. Depending on the type of work that you do working from home might be an option. Discuss with your employer if you could do this part or full time.

5. Use the bus (or a train when travelling off island) rather than your car.

6. Try to reduce the number of flights you take by considering other forms to transport or by using video conferencing for your business meetings.7. Air conditioning can increase your fuel consumption by as much as five per cent. If you need to use air conditioning, try to use it sparingly and otherwise open your windows.

8. Look for lower emission flights, where possible.

9. If you need to fly, consider traveling economy where possible.

10. If you need to fly, consider packing light.

Household Energy & Housing

1. Change your electricity tariff to a low carbon option.

2. Monitor your energy usage.

3. Fit energy energy-efficient light bulbs.

4. Install a new more efficient heating system.

5. Replace your old boiler with a new more energy efficient boiler.

6. Cooking : using a microwave is far more energy efficient than cooking on a traditional gas or electric hob when you’re heating up small amounts of food.

7. When possible, dry clothes outdoors on a washing line which costs nothing and uses no energy. If drying Indoors on a rack you should be mindful of the increase in moisture levels.

8. Buy energy efficient appliances.9. Reduce your hot water usage.

10. Generating your own renewable energy.

Food

1. Reduce food waste.



2. If you have a garden, try growing your own fruit and veg.



3. Avoid the foods that are air-freighted (these tend to be foods which are highly perishable).



4. Buying local can reduce the transport portion of the carbon footprint of your diet but you can have a larger difference by focusing on what you eat.



5. Eating foods that are in season greatly reduces the carbon impact of those foods.

Consumables and Waste

1. Reduce : Buying less stuff will help to reduce your products footprint, whether it is kitchen appliances, clothing or electronic gadgets.



2. Repair : Repair where possible, extending the lifespan of items you own is a great way to reduce your product’s footprint.



3. Reuse : Selling or donating your unwanted things to charity or second-hand organisations helps to extend the useful life of items and keeps them out of landfill.



4. Recycle : Ensure products, packaging and waste are properly recycled.
